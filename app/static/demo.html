Quick tour

One-minute read · everything you need to know.

🔎

Search like you're talking

Type naturally in any of German, French, Italian, or English — we extract the hard constraints (city, price, rooms, bathrooms, cellar, …) and the soft preferences (quiet, near-ETH, commute) automatically.

🔀

Two views, one search

📋 List — the ranker's top matches with full detail, photos, breakdowns, and "why this ranked here". 🗺️ Map — every apartment that passes your filters as a marker on the Swiss map, with neighbourhood clusters and the landmarks you might care about.

Click a map marker to jump to its card. Click a cluster to filter the cards to just that area.

🎛️

Refine without re-typing

The Refine panel on the map lives in two-way sync with your text query. Drag the price or size sliders, toggle "Shared bath" / "Shared kitchen" / "Cellar", or add a landmark — the search re-runs ~300ms later with your new constraints layered on top of the LLM's extraction.

The feature toggles have three states: click once to require, twice to exclude, thrice to reset.

📍

Landmarks as filters

45 hand-picked Swiss places are always on the map — main stations, universities, lakes, employer HQs, old towns. Icons are tinted by category.

Hover — see the landmark's name. Left-click — open its detail popup. Right-click — add it as a "near" filter (the pin glows blue) or remove it if already active.

⭐

Top matches vs. all matches

On the map, bright amber pins with the halo are the ranker's top picks (they also fill the List view). Faded yellow dots are every other apartment that still passes your filters — they're there so you can spot good options the ranker didn't surface. The ⭐ Only top toggle (top-left of the map) hides the faded pool in one click.

📸

Bonus: search by photo

The 📎 button next to the search bar attaches a photo — the site then fuses a DINOv2 visual-similarity ranking into the results, so a picture of "the vibe I want" narrows the listings alongside your text.

🤖 How do we pick great homes for you? (tap to open)
  1. We read your wish. A language model reads what you wrote and splits it into must-haves (like "in Zurich", "under 3000 CHF", "has a balcony") and nice-to-haves (like "quiet", "near a park", "family-friendly").
  2. We remove anything that doesn't fit. Homes that break a must-have are dropped right away — they don't compete for the top spots.
  3. We check the words. We look for the keywords from your wish inside each home's title and description.
  4. We look at the pictures. For each home, we ask: "Do these photos match the kind of home you described?" (a computer-vision model compares your wish to every photo.)
  5. We understand the meaning. We also check whether the description of the home means the same thing as your wish — even when the words are different.
  6. We honour your nice-to-haves. One sorted list per wish — quietest first, or cheapest, or closest to transit, and so on.
  7. We mix the lists together. All those rankings vote for each home; the homes that everyone liked bubble up to the top.
  8. We remember you (only if you let us). If you're logged in and Learn from me is on, we use the homes you loved / saved / hid to nudge the list toward what fits your taste.
